Adherence and the clinical recovery loop

Most people do not realize how tired their eyes are. They adapt, push through, and normalize the strain—until it shows up as poor sleep, reduced focus, and lower daily output. Clinics diagnose this as dry eye disease or digital eye strain and formalize recovery through Umay Rest. Among pilot users, 84% daily adherence was observed. This creates a repeatable loop: diagnosis, prescription, adherence, and reorder—driving durable clinic-level revenue.

Product and regulatory posture

Umay Rest is positioned as a consumer wellness device, manufactured in compliance with applicable Canadian consumer product safety regulations and IEC 60335-1 electrical safety standards. The company uses a four-tier claims ladder with a five-gate evidence system. In plain terms: we say only what the evidence supports, we track what we are allowed to say, and we have a formal process to unlock stronger claims as evidence grows (see Appendix A). This protects regulatory flexibility while maintaining clinical credibility.

RestScan is a browser-based, webcam-powered ocular fatigue and stress assessment tool in development. Designed to run locally with no video storage or facial data transmission. It will add measurement and personalization to the clinic visit, but it is not required for near-term commercial growth. Clinic pilot scheduled ~12 months post-close (see Appendix F for privacy architecture, Appendix O for roadmap).

Three strategic layers

  1. Today — Ocular Recovery Platform: Clinically informed, at-home recovery for dry eye symptoms and digital eye strain, distributed through eye care providers. Current focus: scaling clinic adoption with optimized sales process and repeat reorder behavior.
  2. Next — Closed-Loop Measurement (~12 months post-close): RestScan introduces objective assessment into the clinic visit, converting subjective recommendations into data-supported prescription decisions.
  3. Future — Autonomic Recovery / Neuro-Ocular Health Expansion: Advanced only upon sufficient clinical validation and demonstrated commercial adoption.
Roadmap principle: Scale the clinic-led platform first. Introduce RestScan after clinical validation. Extend into autonomic health only when supported by data and demand.

The thesis in one sentence

Umay built a clinically informed ocular recovery platform with demonstrated clinic reorder behaviour, and is introducing closed-loop measurement to enhance conversion, adherence, and long-term defensibility—while maintaining structured claims governance to preserve full regulatory flexibility.

2. Strategic Thesis and Category Positioning

Section purpose: Why does this category exist, and why is Umay the one to own it? The answer shapes how investors should evaluate the opportunity.

Umay’s strategy starts from the most credible edge: optometric clinical authority paired with a product that already works in market. The company does not “sell wellness into clinics.” It exports clinical credibility into wellness—using clinics as the trust engine, then widening access as logistics and capacity catch up. (See Appendix O for the final category definition, strategic hierarchy, and the hybrid framing: ocular recovery platform first, closed-loop measurement second.)

The foundational insight: under-recognized ocular fatigue. Patients cannot reliably self-assess how strained their eyes are. The damage shows up indirectly—disrupted sleep, reduced focus, diminished daily performance. Clinics are not just buying a device. They are implementing a structured recovery protocol within an unmet and previously unmeasured category. This insight drives product design, channel strategy, and long-term platform expansion.

2.1 Category framing (primary + architectural layer)

Category definition influences how the opportunity is sized and evaluated. Umay should be understood first as an ocular recovery platform—built with optometric clinical insight and engineered therapeutic design—and second as a closed-loop measurement architecture (RestScan) that will strengthen adherence, proof, and personalization as it matures.

  • Ocular Recovery Platform (primary): Umay Rest is an at-home recovery system for ocular comfort and relaxation, aligned to optometry’s daily reality: high prevalence of dry eye symptoms, fatigue complaints, and digital strain. The product integrates into an existing habit window (10 minutes before bed), producing high adherence and low returns. (See Appendix K for channel economics, reorder mechanics, and why clinics function as repeatable distribution nodes.)
  • Closed-loop therapeutic system (architectural layer): RestScan adds an objective measure at the start of the care journey, turning a subjective recommendation into a data-driven prescription moment—without claiming diagnosis. This strengthens conversion, reinforces adherence, and creates a compounding data asset over time. (See Appendix O for the platform architecture and roadmap framing.)

2.2 Tone: quietly confident, clinically grounded, strategically ambitious

Umay is positioned as clinically adjacent wellness: OD-led, science-backed, and compliance-aware—with ambition expressed through defensible execution (patents, clinic adoption, reorder behavior) rather than hype. Rest is the proven core; RestScan is the measurement layer in development that will tighten the loop over time, without being required for near-term success. (See Appendix A for the claims governance ladder and gate system that keeps this posture clean as we scale.)

2.3 The thesis in one sentence

Umay has built a clinically-adjacent ocular recovery platform that clinics already reorder, and is layering in closed-loop measurement to deepen conversion, adherence, and defensibility—while maintaining strict claims discipline to preserve regulatory optionality.

6. Defensibility and Competitive Moat

Section purpose: Why can’t someone else just copy this? Four layers of defense, each reinforcing the others.

Umay’s moat is multi-layered. The strongest strategic moat is clinic distribution with demonstrated reorder behavior—because it is the compounding output of the other moat layers. (See Appendices C, D, and E.)

6.1 Moat layer 1: channel trust + reorder economics (primary moat)

A clinic that has prescribed Umay successfully to dozens of patients has embedded the product into its standard of care. Switching costs are practical and reputational: clinicians do not churn to unproven alternatives when a product is already delivering patient satisfaction and margin. Reorders also reduce dependency on proportional new-clinic acquisition; growth becomes installed-base expansion.

Clinic reorder behaviour functions as more than a revenue signal; it represents behavioural validation. Reorders indicate that Umay has integrated into clinical workflow, demonstrated patient adherence, and generated economic confidence for practitioners. Of Umay’s 42 actively ordering clinics, ~8 Super-Clinics drive approximately 80% of volume, each selling through 5–10 units per month—demonstrating the power law distribution that characterizes successful clinic-dispensed product platforms.

RestScan is the strategic amplifier once it is clinic-ready (~12 months post-close): it can move the recommendation from a subjective complaint to an objective wellness signal at the start of the visit. The core business does not depend on RestScan; it is designed to accelerate conversion, adherence, and reorder once validated and deployed.
