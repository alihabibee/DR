Investment Thesis
A single thermal wearable improved both dry eye symptoms and sleep quality in a government workforce, using validated clinical instruments — demonstrating the bi-directional mechanism that positions Umay at the intersection of a $6B ophthalmic market and a $411B sleep economy.

Study Design

SponsorInnovative Solutions Canada (ISC) / Shared Services Canada
PIDr. Sharmin Habib, OD
DesignProspective, single-arm, pre-post interventional (6 weeks)
Population500 Government of Canada employees (remote, screen-intensive)
InterventionDaily use of the Rest thermal wearable device
AnalysisPaired t-tests (parametric) + Wilcoxon signed-rank (non-parametric); 95% CIs
ControlWithin-subjects (participants as own controls); no external control group (pandemic limitation)
InstrumentsOSDI, CVSS-17, CLDEQ-8, PSQI, STAI
StatusManuscript in draft; targeting peer-reviewed publication
Study limitation: Single-arm design without external control group. Pandemic constraints required within-subjects methodology. Effect sizes and validated instruments provide robust evidence within this design framework.
