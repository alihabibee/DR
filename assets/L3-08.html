What This Pattern Means

The installed base is the growth engine. In 2022, half of all units went to clinics placing their first order. By 2025, first-time clinics account for only 6.1% of volume.

This is the classic SaaS retention curve applied to a hardware channel: acquire once, retain and expand. Each active clinic is an ongoing revenue stream, not a one-time transaction.

Implication for investors: Growth doesn't require proportional new-logo acquisition. Activating the dormant 53% of existing clinics is a faster path to volume than acquiring net-new partners.

2024-2025 data includes backlog fulfillment during extended stockout period. Reorder cadence should be validated post-restock when supply is normalized. The directional pattern (increasing reorder dominance) is robust across all years.

Power Law: Risk or Opportunity?

Concentration risk is real: Top 10 clinics = 58% of units. Loss of any one top partner would be felt.

But the opportunity is larger: The bottom 73 clinics (59% of partners) generate only ~5% of volume. These aren't churned — they're underactivated. Moving even 20 demo/in-office clinics to retail-level purchasing is equivalent to adding 5-10 top accounts without any new-logo acquisition cost.

For in-office clinics (32 at 50% reorder):

The Retention Flywheel

How Clinic Retention Compounds

Patient uses device daily (84%) → Reports improvement to OD at next visit → OD gains confidence → Recommends to more patients → Clinic orders more → Umay ships from inventory → Clinic reorders in ~3 months.

This flywheel is currently running on founder-led relationships and word-of-mouth between ODs. Post-raise, adding dedicated growth staff and RestScan (which makes the improvement measurable) accelerates every stage of the loop.

Key Risks to Retention

Identified Risks

Stockout-induced dormancy: Extended inventory gaps (2024) may have permanently disengaged some clinics.

Concentration dependency: Top 10 clinics = 58% of volume. Relationship risk.

Activation gap: 40 demo clinics (33% of base) have never reordered. May indicate poor first-visit experience or lack of follow-up.

Mitigations

Inventory confidence: $500K from raise allocated to fulfill 1,600-unit backlog. 12 weeks to restock. Eliminates supply uncertainty.

Diversification: Head of Growth hire (US-based) activates new US clinics, reducing Canadian concentration.

Demo conversion: Dedicated onboarding playbook + RestScan installation converts demo clinics to retail. Even 25% conversion = 10 new retail clinics = ~$74K-108K annual margin.
