Why Wellness, Not Medical

Rest is not a medical device because it is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Its positioning focuses on overall wellness and user comfort through a sensory experience inspired by science-based thermal therapy methods.

This mirrors the Oura approach: wellness positioning with clinical validation building underneath. It preserves iteration speed while accumulating evidence for a future regulatory upgrade.

General Wellness guidance (FDA, 2016/2019): devices intended for general wellness use that make low-risk claims are not subject to device regulation.

The 5-Gate System: How Claims Graduate

Claims cannot move from a lower tier to a higher tier without passing through specific evidence gates. This prevents scope creep and ensures regulatory integrity.

1
Internal Signal
Observational data suggests a relationship. Internal use only.
2
Pilot Validation
Prospective study with validated instruments. N≥50. Pre/post design minimum.
3
Peer Review
Published in indexed journal. External review complete.
4
Regulatory Filing
510(k), De Novo, or equivalent submission with supporting evidence.
5
Cleared Claim
Regulatory body grants clearance for the specific indication.
Where Umay Currently Sits: Gate 2 (passed). The ISC/SSC pilot (N=500, 5 validated instruments, statistical significance across all domains) has cleared the pilot validation gate. Manuscript targeting Gate 3 (peer review). Gate 4-5 reserved for future medical device claims.
RestScan Gate Position: Gate 1 (internal signal). N=50 validation study underway (85% Oura correlation target + OSDI + TBUT). Must clear Gate 2 before any clinical claims. Current positioning: wellness measurement tool, not clinical diagnostic.
Section 3 · Regulatory Pathway Strategy

Phased Regulatory Architecture

Umay maintains wellness positioning today while building the evidentiary foundation for medical claims tomorrow. Each phase preserves optionality without triggering premature regulatory burden.

Phase 1 · Now (Active)
Consumer Wellness — Class I Exempt

Rest device: "Promotes relaxation and comfort." No disease claims. Health Canada approved. IEC 60335-1 certified. General Wellness guidance compliant (FDA 2016/2019). Exploring 513(g) assessment to formally confirm Class I / 510(k) exempt status for dry eye comfort indication.

Phase 2 · Near-Term (Post-Publication)
Evidence-Supported Wellness

Habib et al. publication elevates pilot claims from Tier 2 → Tier 1 (published, peer-reviewed). Enables stronger positioning: "Proven in peer-reviewed study to improve eye comfort and sleep quality." Still within wellness framework — no disease-specific treatment claims.

Phase 3 · Post-Raise (12-18 months)
Class I / 510(k) for DED Comfort

513(g) assessment confirms classification. If Class I exempt: continue current path with documented compliance. If 510(k) required: submit with ISC/SSC data + additional US study. Enables "indicated for dry eye symptom relief" positioning.

Phase 4 · Future (18+ months)
De Novo for Sleep Indications

FDA De Novo classification for "Ocular-mediated Sleep Improvement" — a novel category. Requires: dedicated sleep study (RCT design), predicate analysis confirming no existing classification, demonstrated safety + efficacy for sleep-specific claims. This unlocks medical-grade positioning and potential insurance reimbursement.

SaMD Decision Framework (RestScan)

Currently NOT SaMD

RestScan in its current form is not Software as a Medical Device because:

→ It does not provide disease diagnosis or treatment recommendations
→ It provides general wellness information ("fatigue score")
→ It does not claim to inform clinical decision-making
→ Output is informational to the user, not prescriptive

Would Become SaMD If…

RestScan would trigger SaMD classification if:

→ It claimed to diagnose DED or any specific condition
→ It recommended specific treatment protocols based on scan results
→ ODs used scan output as a primary diagnostic input
→ It generated reports labeled as "clinical assessments"

Mitigation: All RestScan outputs are labeled as "wellness insights" not "clinical findings." Clinical claims governance (Tier 3+) requires formal gate passage.
