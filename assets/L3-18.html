The portfolio covers both the therapeutic device (Rest) and the measurement platform (RestScan), creating IP protection across both pillars of the closed-loop system. Why the Stress-Sensing Patent Matters Most

The November 2025 patent protects the integration of physiological stress detection with adaptive thermal therapy. This is the IP foundation for the closed-loop system (RestScan measures → Rest treats → RestScan re-measures). Any competitor attempting to build a similar detect-treat loop would need to either license this patent or design around it — a significant barrier given the specificity of the periocular application.

IP Strategy & Competitive Moat Architecture

Three-Layer IP Defense

Layer 1: Patents

Utility patents protect the device architecture and the critical stress-sensing / closed-loop integration. Provisionals extend coverage to RestScan (measurement) and Thermal Rhythm (adaptive protocols). Creates legal barriers to direct copying.

Layer 2: Data

N=500 pilot data, 2,847 active IoT devices generating longitudinal usage data, and RestScan validation datasets. This data advantage compounds over time — every patient interaction widens the gap. New entrants start with zero clinical evidence.

Layer 3: Channel

134 clinic relationships with proven reorder behavior (66.7% retail reorder rate). Clinical channel trust takes years to build — an OD who has successfully recommended Umay to 50+ patients won't switch to an unproven competitor.
